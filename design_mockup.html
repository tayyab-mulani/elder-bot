What is the 2% Rule in risk management?
AE
Elder  ·  4 sources retrieved
The 2% Rule is one of Elder's core risk management principles: never risk more than 2% of your total trading account on any single trade.

For a $10,000 account, no single stop-loss should result in a loss greater than $200. This creates a statistical safety net — you can lose 10 trades in a row and still preserve 82% of your capital. Elder considers this the dividing line between gambling and professional trading.

How does the Triple Screen system decide when to enter a trade?
AE
Elder  ·  4 sources retrieved
Entry requires all three screens to agree simultaneously:

Screen 1 — Trend (EMA): EMA₁₅ must cross above EMA₂₀₀, confirming the market is in an uptrend on the weekly timeframe.
Screen 2 — Momentum (MACD): MACD line must be above its signal line, confirming the trend has momentum behind it.
Screen 3 — Timing (RSI): RSI must dip below the lower bound (default 50), signalling a brief pullback within the trend — your precise entry window.
